# English delivery translation and detailed summary

Source: Italian transcript of a public speech in Collesano, duration about 97 minutes, transcribed on 2026-06-22.

Note: This is a spoken-style English translation. It preserves the meaning, tone, and main claims, but removes many repetitions, filler words, false starts, and transcription noise.

## English delivery translation

There are more of you here in the square than I expected, and that makes me very happy. It shows that in this town there are still people who want to think with their own heads, after understanding how things really are.

Why am I here? Because the first video and the first document published by the administration and the mayor contains falsehoods about two strategic issues for this community: the wastewater treatment plant and the Community House. These are not minor matters. They concern the future of Collesano itself.

On the wastewater treatment plant, the mayor wrote that no funding had been assigned, that there was only an open procedure, and that the services conference with ATI and the Regional Department for Water and Waste had not been concluded during my mandate. Part of that is a play on words. But the claim that the project was updated only later is, in my view, a huge falsehood, and I will prove it with documents that will be published for everyone to read.

The other issue is even more serious: the Community House. Only two Community Houses in all of Sicily were lost, and one of them was Collesano. The mayor says there was no municipal investment. Technically that is true, because the investment was regional, but the Region had identified Collesano as a location. It could and should have had 15 medical clinics and a health service open 24 hours a day. Imagine what that would have meant for a community with many elderly people.

I served two terms as mayor. Anyone who has had the honor of representing a community has a duty to face the people and explain how things really happened. Being mayor does not end the day after you leave office. You do not switch off your civil and social responsibility toward a town you cared for. A real mayor tells the truth, and if he makes a mistake, he accepts responsibility. He does not deny the evidence or push the blame onto others.

I supported the current administration. I could have run again, but I chose not to. I supported a younger team that had been close to me, believing they would continue the work already started. I went to the homes of friends and voters and asked them to vote for them. I thought they would at least carry forward the projects that had already been created. Some projects are completed during a mandate, but others, because of bureaucracy, must be completed by the following administration.

After they won, I expected at least respect and recognition. Instead, I felt betrayed. From that moment, I understood that they had decided to abandon Giovanni Meli. Still, I hoped they would at least continue the important projects: the Community House, the wastewater treatment plant, and the urban plan.

I warned them. Months before deadlines expired, I made videos, wrote posts, and contacted the minority councillors, because the administration would not speak to me. I told them to raise the issues officially in the municipal council. I warned them that if certain steps were not taken, the town would lose these projects. Despite the warnings, despite the council questions, despite my posts, they lost them.

### Wastewater treatment plant

Let us start with the wastewater treatment plant. The mayor says there was no funding and no real call. But I refer to Ministerial Decree No. 191 of 17 May 2022, Mission M2C4, Investment 4.4, concerning sewerage and wastewater treatment. This decree set the criteria for distributing resources: 600 million euros nationally. It opened the door for Collesano to present a project and access those funds.

On 17 July 2022, the Madonie Development Agency wrote to the Municipality of Collesano about the approval of the allocation criteria for resources dedicated to sewerage and wastewater treatment. The letter said that the Ministry had assigned 61,632,000 euros for those investments and asked municipalities to provide data quickly so that a meeting with the technical structure could be scheduled.

At that time, Collesano had an old wastewater plant and the Region was asking municipalities to make upgrades. I had given an assignment for upgrade works costing around 250,000 euros, which would have had to be paid by the community from the municipal surplus. Then we had the opportunity not only to do upgrades, but to seek funding for a new wastewater plant.

The relevant note said that the project could not be below 1.5 million euros. So the opportunity was clear: instead of spending 250,000 euros of local money for upgrades, Collesano could pursue a new plant at no cost to the community. I asked the technical office to proceed officially. The technical studio worked quickly because there was a deadline. The project reached about 1.409 million euros and was submitted to ATI for the necessary steps.

The mayor now plays with words by saying the funding was not directly assigned to the municipality. But the money was available through ATI: the municipality had to submit the project in order to access it.

Then something incredible happened. The technical studio that had prepared the larger project asked to be paid. The administration refused. The studio initiated an injunction against the municipality. The municipality hired a lawyer to oppose the claim, spending more public money. The legal documents, according to the speaker, show that the work had been officially requested and that the project had grown from a 250,000-euro upgrade into a 1.409-million-euro project for the wastewater plant.

There was also a technical issue: ATI argued that a change in urban planning designation was required. The speaker argues that this should not have been necessary because the new plant was to be built on the same site as the existing plant. Even if the issue existed, the administration had a year and a half to solve it. A mayor should have pushed through the services conference, gone to ATI, involved the regional assessor or even the regional president if necessary. Instead, Collesano lost the chance to get a new wastewater plant, and now the necessary upgrades must be paid from local money.

### Urban plan / Piano Regolatore

The urban plan had been in progress for around ten years, with assignments, technical work, regulatory updates, and about 100,000 euros of public money already spent. The draft had been brought to the municipal council before I left office. Collesano was under a transitional regime that allowed the old planning process to be completed, but only by 31 December 2025.

On 11 September 2025, I made a public post warning that silence had fallen over the urban plan. I said clearly that the final technical and bureaucratic steps had to be completed by 31 December 2025, otherwise the town would lose the work already done, the public money already spent, and the opportunity to have a fundamental planning tool.

According to the speaker, the administration ignored the warning. The minority also raised the issue in the municipal council, but nothing happened. The deadline passed. The old plan was lost. Now the municipality must start again under the new rules, with new assignments, new costs of at least around 150,000 euros, and likely another long bureaucratic process.

The speaker stresses that the urban plan was not an abstract document. It addressed the economy, work, future productive activities, craft and commercial zones, accommodation facilities, public services, roads, parking, social spaces, schools, culture, and protection of historical and artistic heritage. It defined the needs and aspirations of the community and guided how public resources, regional funds, national funds, and European funds should be used.

### Community House / Casa di Comunità

The speaker says the Community House was the strongest issue. He says Collesano had been included in a regional health plan as a location for a Community House. The building identified was the former small hospital / Opera Pia area, owned by the ASP, in Via della Croce, cadastral sheet 36, parcel 977.

He reads from an official letter from technicians appointed by ASP Palermo. The letter asks the Municipality of Collesano for copies of all building permits and documents relating to the property so they could proceed with the design of the new Community House of Collesano. The speaker says this proves the project was real and not his fantasy.

He also refers to WhatsApp communication with engineer Cristiano Biello / Bilendo. On 17 November 2024, after Meli was no longer mayor, he asked for updates. The engineer replied that they had stopped at the technical and economic feasibility project and that he did not know how ASP had continued. He also stated that the allocated value for the Collesano Community House was 500,000 euros.

The speaker admits 500,000 euros may have been too little for a structure with 15 medical clinics and 24-hour service. But he argues that this was exactly when the mayor should have gone to Palermo, to the Region and the relevant assessor, and asked for additional funding. According to him, not all Community House funds were spent, so additional resources could have been requested.

Instead, he says, the project was neglected and finally suppressed by official decree. If there had truly been nothing, he asks, why would there have been a decree including Collesano and later another decree suppressing Collesano’s Community House? He says the funds intended for Collesano were redistributed to other municipalities.

### Other projects and Opera Pia

The speaker also claims that many projects the current administration presents as its own were actually prepared during his administration: public lighting, road works, the sports field, rockfall protection, Opera Pia, and others.

He mentions a 3.5-million-euro hydrogeological safety project for the rocky slopes / historic center area, handled through the Commissioner for Hydrogeological Emergency in Palermo. He says the current administration did not properly follow it until he publicly warned that it too was at risk.

On Opera Pia and social-health services, he says the current administration is now promoting a project as if it were theirs, but the original funding and project came from his mandate. He cites Decree No. 440 of 9 December 2022 for almost 2 million euros, with Collesano as lead municipality together with Gangi and Gratteri, under a public call for community social infrastructure and inclusion services. He says this project includes 30 months of support for elderly and vulnerable people.

### Doctor Loredana Di Santo’s intervention

Doctor Loredana Di Santo then speaks. She says she is emotional to return to the square and to the town where she worked for nearly ten years. She explains that Community Houses are part of the reorganization of territorial healthcare under the PNRR and are an example of LEA, essential levels of care.

She explains why such a structure matters. If a patient has an allergic reaction after the family doctor’s office is closed, today the patient may have to go to the emergency room, even when it is not a true emergency. With a Community House, the patient could go there and find a multidisciplinary team. There would be medical presence during the day, and continuity-of-care / guardia medica service during the night.

She says a Community House also includes the family nurse, specialists, support for elderly and fragile patients, CUP booking services, chronic disease management, and home-based integrated care. She gives examples: diabetes, heart failure, respiratory insufficiency, and patients discharged after a femur fracture who need wound care, rehabilitation, and hygiene support.

She says people often lose weeks in bureaucracy and sometimes must pay privately, even though care is a right. Collesano also deserves this right, not only big cities. She also mentions prevention and screening, saying real therapy is prevention.

She ends by saying the town has lost a great opportunity, although it can at least take comfort in the other 30-month social-services project prepared under the former administration.

### Final appeal

The speaker closes by asking people not to accept excuses such as “there were logistical problems” or “the building was not suitable.” He says the original area was good, with road access and parking, and that an alternative such as the former slaughterhouse would not have been suitable because of narrow roads and access problems.

He calls for a renewed effort. He says there may be new resources and a second chance for Community Houses, and that from the next morning the mayor, the minority, and everyone involved should request an official meeting about those resources. The message is: never give up when a project this important is at stake.

## Detailed summary

### Main message

The speech is a public accusation against the current Collesano administration. The speaker, former mayor Giovanni Meli, argues that the administration has lost or neglected several strategic projects that were prepared or started under his mandate. He says the current mayor and administration are misleading citizens by saying that those projects did not really exist or were not funded.

The tone is confrontational, emotional, and strongly political. The speaker repeatedly says he will publish documents so citizens can judge for themselves.

### Medical explanation by Dr. Loredana Di Santo

Doctor Loredana Di Santo explains what the Community House would have meant in practical terms:
- It is part of the PNRR territorial healthcare reform.
- It is linked to LEA, essential levels of care.
- It would reduce unnecessary emergency-room visits.
- It would provide day medical service plus night continuity care.
- It would include family nurses, doctors, specialists, booking services, chronic disease management, and home care.
- It would help elderly and fragile patients.
- It would support prevention and screening.
- She stresses that healthcare is a right and that Collesano deserves these services too.

### Political framing

The speaker frames the current administration as focused on events, ceremonies, flags, festivals, Miss World, Targa Florio, Epiphany and Carnival, while allegedly neglecting structural projects that affect daily life.

He says people are afraid to speak openly or even like posts on social media because they fear pressure or retaliation.

He repeatedly says he does not want an official role or appointment, but as former mayor he feels responsible for defending projects started during his mandate.

### Final request / call to action

The final appeal is that Collesano should not give up. The speaker says there may be new resources and a second chance for the Community House. He calls on the mayor, the minority, and citizens to request an official meeting with the relevant regional authorities and try again.
